A rectangle is a 2D shape in geometry, having 4 sides and 4 corners. Its two sides meet at right angles. Thus, a rectangle has 4 angles, each measuring 90 °. The opposite sides of a rectangle have the same lengths and are parallel.

A square is formed with four equal or roughly equal sides and four right angles.

In geometry, a circle is a perfectly round shape—meaning any point around its curve is the same distance from its central point.

A triangle is a three-sided polygon that closes in a space. It uses lines, line segments or rays (in any combination) to form the three sides. When three sides form and meet, they create three vertices, or corners.
